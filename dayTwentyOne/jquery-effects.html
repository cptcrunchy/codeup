Arches

  • On April 12, 1929, Arches became a National Monument.
  • Arches Visitor Center sits at just over 4,000 feet (1,219 m) in elevation.
  • The light opening beneath Delicate Arch is 46 feet (14 m) high.
  • Most of the sandstones in the park contain iron oxide.

Badlands

  • The name 'badlands' is believed to originate from the Lakota people.
  • President Franklin D. Roosevelt created Badlands National Monument by signing a proclamation.
  • Congress declared the monument Badlands National Park in 1978.
  • Snakes, including the prairie rattlesnake, are common in Badlands National Park.

Denali

  • Denali's peak is 20,310 feet above sea level, making it the highest mountain in North America.
  • Denali is located in Denali National Park and Preserve, which is more than 6 million acres in size.
  • n 1896 Denali was renamed Mount McKinley.
  • In 1975 the mountain was officially named Denali by the state of Alaska.
